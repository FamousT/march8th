THE MARCH 8TH INITIATIVE;
MSME GRANT FOR SMALL BUSINESSES

Entrepreneurship plays a vital role in creating opportunity and livelihoods now and in the future. The Nigerian spirit of enterprise is inimitable and is recognised as the pivot for growth in our communities and the national economy. And young Nigerians are the lifeblood of Nigerian enterprise. The energy, enthusiasm, and innovation of our significant youth generation, if unleashed, can result in substantial benefits for our nation and her people.

Many progressive minded Nigerians are continually inspired by the vision, passion and commitment of Prof. Osinbajo, to the causes of small businesses, young people, technology and employment. Prof has been a crusader for the renaissance of MSMEs in Nigeria and a champion for the mainstreaming of pro-poor developmental polices.

The grant was instituted to commemorate the birthday of “Prof” as he is fondly referred to by many. This initiative is about creating opportunities and pathways for young people to find viable expression in entrepreneurship. This is why the “Friends of Prof” who share a similar conviction about Nigerian youth and entrepreneurship as Prof, have galvanised resources to create this opportunity to coincide with his birthday on March 8th 2020. The central idea is to provide, as a first step, small business grants to young Nigerians seeking opportunities for start-up, growth and expansion.

The Screening Process

The 2021 Process

The March 8th Initiative which kicked off last year with over 26,000 applications is back again this year to give one-off business grants, between N100,000 and N1,000,000 to small businesses in Nigeria, to commemorate the birthday of His Excellency, Prof. Yemi Osinbajo, SAN, GCON. This year’s instalment of the initiative features four broad categories that cover varying stages of small business growth, targeting budding ideas, early growth, catalyst stage and innovation in tech and Agro enterprises. Small and medium business owners in and around Nigeria are invited to apply across the following four grant categories:

THE IDEA STAGE GRANT

Do you have a small-scale business, or an idea you are about to launch? Record a 90-seconds video describing what your business idea is about, for a chance to win a N100,000 grant. The video can be recorded in English, pidgin, Ibo, Yoruba or Hausa language.

Entry Criteria

1. The video entry should start with the applicant’s full name and location stated clearly.

2. The video entry should show the applicant describing the business idea and how the grant will help the business launch.

3. The video entry should be uploaded on either Facebook, Twitter or Instagram using the hashtag #FriendsOfProf, and tag @march8thng

THE EARLY STAGE GRANT

The early-stage grant category targets small businesses that are in need of capital infusion for stocking and to prove viability, and offers a N250, 000 grant as support for that stage of entrepreneurship.

Entry Criteria

1. The business must not have been founded longer than 1 year.

2. The entry requirement is a one-page business proposal, not exceeding 500 words, describing your business and how a sum of N250, 000 will help it grow.

THE CATALYST GRANT

The N500,000 grant category is for existing businesses looking to expand and create new products or services to stimulate growth, in line with their business objective.

Entry Criteria

1. The Business must be between 2 – 3 years old at the time of the application.

2. Entry requirement is a business proposal not exceeding 750 words, describing your existing business and the new products or services you want to venture into and how you plan to leverage existing infrastructure.

THE TECH & AGRO GRANT

This grant category is offering a N1 Million grant for bold and innovative tech or Agro ideas that not only departs from the status quo but has a high potential for impact, across an entire community, city, or country.

Entry Criteria

1. Write a proposal not exceeding 1000 words on how N1 Million will help your business create innovative tech or Agro solutions in Nigeria.

APPLICATION ELIGIBILITY FOR ALL GRANT CATEGORIES.

Interested applicants must

1. Reside in Nigeria

2. Be aged between 18 to 35 years by March 8th 2021

3. Be a Nigerian Brand
